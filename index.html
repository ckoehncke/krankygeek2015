About the Kranky Geek WebRTC event

This event is a collaboration been the Chris "Kranky" Koehncke (www.chriskranky.com) and Tsahi Levi-Levent (BlogGeek.me) and Chad Hart (www.webrtchacks.com) have been passionate bloggers on the topic of WebRTC.

We participate in events aroun the world but felt these events weren't inspiring a new era of thinking about communications nor the underlying power of WebRTC. This San Francisco event targeted to the HTML5 developer looking to learn more about WebRTC and create the inspiration for a next generation of web applications with a bi-directional communications path.

Why attend?

Here's why you should attend this event

WebRTC isn't widely understood, but it has some powerful capabilities to change the way we communicate, not just between ourselves but to change the entire experience on the web. The objective of this event is to (1) provide technical knowledge on the construct of WebRTC, (2) show you real life examples of how to embed into your application, (3) inspire you with new ideas from our speakers and finally (4) the future of WebRTC as it hits mobile devices.

WebRTC is continuing to evolve and we'll discuss ORTC (Object Real Time Communications) which represents the forward movement of WebRTC toward a web standard.

The event on Friday, September 11th 2015 and this is the 2nd annual Kranky Geek event on WebRTC at Google. We listened to your comments from last year and this year you'll find more technical sessions and information on how to actually implement WebRTC into your application.
