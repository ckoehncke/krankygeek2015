About the Kranky Geek WebRTC event

Chris "Kranky" Koehncke (www.chriskranky.com) and Tsahi Levi-Levent (bloggeek.me) have been two passionate bloggers on the topic of WebRTC.

Both are sought after speakers at WebRTC events around the world. However,we felt these events weren't inspiring a new era of thinking about communications nor the underlying power of WebRTC - they focus on VoIP instead of the web. This San Francisco event, is a first and is solely targeted to the HTML5 developer looking to learn more about WebRTC and create the inspiration for a next generation of web applications with a bi-directional communications path.

Why attend?

Here's why you should attend this event

We've attended a lot of WebRTC events in the past year and we always felt something was missing. It was a classic Goldilocks problem, events were either to immersed in talking about standards & technology (or not enough). Too commercial with light n' fluffy content or too dark. Something was missing. We wanted to be inspired.


After talking with others, we realized we're weren't alone in thinking this. The kind folks at Google echo'd some similar feelings and they urged us to up the game by putting together an event with topics that we wanted. Thus this Kranky Geek event was borne.


The event on Friday, 27. June 2014 is neighboring the massive Google I/O conference so we're hoping you're in town and in the mood to learn more about WebRTC, what you can do with it, how to make it work for your application, hear what others have done and learn about the future direction of web communications. It's a lot to cover in a 1/2 day session, but we're aiming to inspire you.
